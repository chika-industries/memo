### Scene 2: Depreciation

**Setting:** Monk's Diner. Jerry and Elaine are having coffee. Kramer walks in, looking distressed.

**Kramer:** It's gone. It's just... gone.

**Elaine:** What? What's gone? Your mind? Because that's been missing for years.

**Kramer:** My car! My beautiful 1978 LeMans! I left it on the street for two weeks, and when I came back... it was worth $200 less!

**Jerry:** So... someone stole $200 from your car? They broke in and took your ashtray money?

**Kramer:** No! No one stole anything! It's just worth less! The paint faded a little. The tires lost some air. The universe just reached down and said, "Kramer, your car is now worth $200 less." And I looked it up! There's a name for it! Depreciation!

**Elaine:** Yeah, Kramer, that's what happens. Cars lose value over time. It's not the universe, it's just... reality.

**Kramer:** No, no, no. It's an accounting principle! And if it's an accounting principle, there must be a place I can go to get that value back! Some kind of... Depreciation Reimbursement Bureau!

**Jerry:** You think there's a government office where you can file a claim for the natural, inevitable aging of your possessions? "Excuse me, my shoes are a little scuffed. I'd like to file for scuff-reciation."

**Kramer:** Why not?! The government tracks everything else! They should track this! It's a loss! An involuntary loss of value! I want my $200!

**Elaine:** From who? The weather? The passage of time? Are you gonna sue Thursday?

**Kramer:** *(Pacing, getting excited)* Maybe not the government... maybe the *manufacturer*! They built it to depreciate! Planned obsolescence! It's a conspiracy! Those General Motors fat cats are sitting in their ivory towers, laughing at my shrinking resale value! I'm gonna write them a letter! A strongly worded letter!

**Jerry:** You do that. And while you're at it, see if you can get them to pay you for the emotional depreciation of having to drive a 1978 LeMans. That car was ugly when it was new.

**Kramer:** *(Pointing at Jerry)* That's it! Emotional depreciation! Pain and suffering of the vehicle! I'm adding it to the letter! This could be big, Jerry! Big!

*(Kramer rushes out, energized.)*

**Jerry:** He's going to be so disappointed when he realizes the only depreciation he can claim is on his tax return, and it's a benefit, not a payout.

**Elaine:** You wanna tell him?

**Jerry:** No way. I want to see how many stamps he uses first.
