### Scene 7: Accrual Accounting

**Setting:** Monk's Diner. Jerry is at the counter. Kramer slides into the seat next to him, looking smug and holding a napkin covered in scribbles.

**Kramer:** Jerry. I've made a discovery that's going to change everything. Everything.

**Jerry:** Is it that the tuna salad here has been the same color for three years and that might be a bad sign?

**Kramer:** No, no. Accrual accounting. It's the secret sauce of the big corporations. And I've just applied it to my life.

**Jerry:** You applied corporate accounting principles to your existence? What could possibly go wrong?

**Kramer:** Listen! Cash accounting is for amateurs. That's when you only count money when it actually hits your hand. Small thinking, Jerry. Small. But accrual accounting? You count revenue when it's *earned*, not when you get paid! And you count expenses when they're *incurred*, not when you pay them!

**Jerry:** Okay... so how does this work in Kramer-world?

**Kramer:** *(Pulls out the napkin)* Last week, I helped an old lady carry her groceries up three flights of stairs. That's a service performed! Revenue earned! So I've put down $20 on my napkin here as "Accounts Receivable - Grocery Lady."

**Jerry:** Did she *agree* to pay you $20?

**Kramer:** She gave me a lovely smile and said "God bless you, young man." That's a verbal contract, Jerry. Binding.

**Jerry: That's not a contract. That's just a nice moment.**

**Kramer:** Also, I found a parking spot right in front of my building yesterday. Prime real estate! That's a benefit received! So I've accrued $15 of value from the universe.

**Jerry:** You're accruing money from a parking spot.

**Kramer:** And here's the beautiful part! I went to the deli this morning and got a sandwich. I didn't pay yet. But under accrual accounting, I've *incurred* the expense! So I owe them $8. That's a liability. So my net income on the week? *(He does some napkin math)* Twenty plus fifteen minus eight... Jerry, I'm up $27 and I haven't collected a dime! I'm profitable on paper!

**Jerry:** So according to your napkin, you're doing great. Meanwhile, in reality, you have no money, you owe the deli $8, and you're going to be very confused when the grocery lady doesn't mail you a check.

**Kramer:** The numbers don't lie, Jerry! This is how Enron did it! Wait, bad example. This is how Amazon does it! They're always losing money on paper but they're actually huge!

**Jerry:** They're losing money on *purpose* because they reinvest everything. You're "making" money on purpose because you're imagining it. There's a difference.

**Kramer:** I'm going to the bank tomorrow to apply for a loan. I'm bringing my napkin. They're gonna see $27 in accrued profits and they're gonna say, "Kramer, you're a financial genius. Here's $10,000."

**Jerry:** They're gonna say, "Sir, this is a napkin with crayon on it. Please leave."

**Kramer:** *(Ignoring him, lost in thought)* I could also accrue some rental income from that spot on my couch where Newman sits. He's always there. That's basically a tenant. I'm leaving money on the table, Jerry!

**Jerry:** You're leaving reality. Far behind. I can barely see it from here.

*(The waiter comes over with the check.)*

**Waiter:** Here you go, Jerry. Kramer, you want anything?

**Kramer:** I'll have a coffee. Put it on my tab.

**Jerry:** You don't have a tab.

**Kramer:** I'm accruing one!

*(The waiter looks at Jerry, who just sighs and nods.)*

**Jerry:** Just put it on mine. Consider it a goodwill impairment charge.
