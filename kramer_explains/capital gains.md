### Scene 6: Capital Gains

**Setting:** Jerry's apartment. Jerry is watching TV. Kramer bursts in, dragging a large, hideous painting of clowns playing poker.

**Kramer:** Jerry! I'm retiring! I'm done! Finished! You're looking at a man of leisure!

**Jerry:** You're retiring from what? The profession of bursting through my door unannounced? Because you're really at the top of your game.

**Kramer:** No, no! I'm retiring on my capital gains! *(He leans the painting against the couch)*

**Jerry:** *(Staring at the painting)* Is that... is that a clown cheating? He's hiding an ace in his ruffled sleeve.

**Kramer:** It's called "Poker Faces," by an artist named Vincent. I bought it at a garage sale last week for $20. And today, Jerry... today it's worth $500!

**Jerry:** According to who? The guy who painted it? Because he's the only one who would pay $500 for that nightmare fuel.

**Kramer:** According to *me*! I'm the appraiser! And I've decided it's a capital asset. I've held it for more than a year—

**Jerry:** You've held it for a week.

**Kramer:** In dog years! Look, the point is, when I sell it, I'll have a capital gain of $480! And capital gains are taxed at a lower rate than regular income! It's the rich guy loophole, Jerry! I'm in the club!

**Jerry:** You haven't sold it. You haven't even *tried* to sell it. You just walked in here and declared victory. That's not a capital gain, that's a capital delusion.

**Kramer:** I'm about to sell it! Right now! To you! It's the perfect piece for over your couch. Really ties the room together. Clowns, Jerry. Poker. High society. Think about it.

**Jerry:** I am thinking about it. I'm thinking about how that clown's eyes follow me around the room. I'm thinking about the nightmares. I'm thinking about the $500 I would not have.

**Kramer:** But think of the tax advantages! You buy it for $500, then next year you sell it to someone else for $1,000! Capital gains! You'll thank me!

**Jerry:** So your plan is for me to buy this painting from you so that *I* can have the capital gain later, which means *you* don't get any money now except the $500 I give you, which is exactly what you wanted in the first place, except now you've introduced a middleman and a fantasy about the future?

**Kramer:** *(Pausing)* ...It's a partnership, Jerry. You bring the capital, I bring the gain. We're in this together.

**Jerry:** The only thing we're in together is this conversation, and I'm trying to get out of it. Take your clown mafia and go.

**Kramer:** *(Not moving, staring at the painting)* You know, the more I look at this, the more I think $500 might be too low. This is a masterpiece. The emotion! The tension! That cheating clown is really sticking it to the system. I identify with him.

**Jerry:** Of course you do.

**Kramer:** I'm raising the price to $600. No, $700! The capital gains are just gonna keep growing!

**Jerry:** That's not how any of this works. You can't just invent value because you identify with a cheating clown.

**Kramer:** Watch me! I'm gonna go see if Newman wants to get in on the ground floor. This could be bigger than beanie babies, Jerry! Bigger than pet rocks! This is... clown capitalism!

*(Kramer drags the painting out, nearly taking out the doorframe.)*

**Jerry:** *(To himself)* Clown capitalism. And people wonder why I don't invest.
