### Scene 13: Hedging

**Setting:** Jerry's apartment. Jerry is watching TV. Kramer bursts in, but something is different. He's wearing a raincoat, carrying an umbrella, and has a thermometer hanging around his neck.

**Jerry:** Whoa. What is this? Are you auditioning for a community theater production of "The Weatherman"?

**Kramer:** Jerry, I've discovered the ultimate financial safety net. It's called hedging. And I'm now fully hedged against all of life's uncertainties!

**Jerry:** Hedged? You look like you're preparing for a hurricane that only hits your apartment.

**Kramer:** That's the genius of it! You identify your risks, and then you take offsetting positions! It's what the big commodities traders do! Wheat futures, oil futures, orange juice futures—they never sleep, Jerry!

**Jerry:** You're not a commodities trader. You're a guy who sells hats out of his apartment. Badly.

**Kramer:** Exactly! And what's my biggest risk? The weather! If it rains, nobody comes to look at my hats! My outdoor hat display is useless! So I've hedged!

**Jerry:** How do you hedge against rain? Do you have a contract with the sun?

**Kramer:** Better! I bought 500 umbrellas!

*(Kramer pulls out a cheap, folded umbrella from his coat.)*

**Jerry:** You bought umbrellas.

**Kramer:** Five hundred of them! From a guy in the garment district who was going out of business. If it rains, people need umbrellas! I'll sell umbrellas instead of hats! My portfolio is balanced!

**Jerry:** So now you have 5,000 hats AND 500 umbrellas. Your apartment must look like a lost and found for a clown convention that got caught in a monsoon.

**Kramer:** It's a little tight, yeah. But I'm protected! If hats go down, umbrellas go up! That's hedging!

**Jerry:** That's not hedging. That's just having two things you can't sell instead of one.

**Kramer:** No, no, you're missing the correlation! Hats and umbrellas are negatively correlated! When one is up, the other is down! I'm immune to weather fluctuations!

**Jerry:** You're immune to selling anything. That's your only constant.

---

**Setting:** Kramer's apartment. One week later. It's a disaster zone. Hats everywhere. Umbrellas everywhere. Kramer is sitting in the middle, surrounded by chaos, talking to Newman.

**Newman:** Kramer, this is a catastrophe. You owe me money, and now you've filled the remaining space with rain protection. I can't even get to your fridge to take my daily hat collateral.

**Kramer:** I know, I know! But the hedge should have worked! If it rained, I'd sell umbrellas! But it hasn't rained in eight days! Sunny and 75 every day! The umbrella market is dead!

**Newman:** So your hedge failed because the weather was too nice.

**Kramer:** It's unprecedented! Who ordered this beautiful weather? I certainly didn't!

**Newman:** What about the hats? Any sales?

**Kramer:** A theater group bought twelve for a production of "Steel Magnolias: The Musical." But they paid in exposure. Exposure, Newman! I can't pay you with exposure!

**Newman:** I don't want exposure. I want cash. Or hats. At this point, mostly hats.

**Kramer:** *(Suddenly inspired)* Wait a minute! What if I need to hedge against *Newman*?

**Newman:** What?

**Kramer:** You're a risk, Newman! You're a variable! If I could hedge against you, I'd be truly protected!

**Newman:** How would you hedge against a person?

**Kramer:** I don't know... buy insurance? Take out a contract? Befriend someone bigger than you who owes me a favor?

**Newman:** You're describing a bodyguard, Kramer. That's not a hedge, that's a thug.

**Kramer:** It's all the same in the risk management world, Newman! Offsetting positions!

*(Jerry appears in the doorway, surveying the chaos.)*

**Jerry:** I knocked. There's no door anymore. It's just hats and umbrellas. I had to swim through them.

**Kramer:** Jerry! Tell Newman about hedging! He doesn't understand modern finance!

**Jerry:** I understand that you now have two unsellable inventories instead of one. That's not hedging. That's diversifying your failure. It's like falling down two sets of stairs instead of one.

**Kramer:** But I'm protected!

**Jerry:** From what? Success? Because you've successfully created a situation where no matter what happens, you lose. If it rains, you have umbrellas but nobody's outside to buy hats or umbrellas because they're all inside avoiding the rain. If it's sunny, nobody needs umbrellas. You've hedged yourself into a corner where every outcome is bad.

**Newman:** He's right, Kramer. Your hedge is actually a double-loss guarantee.

**Kramer:** *(Pause, then slowly)* ...So what you're saying is, I need to hedge against my hedging.

**Jerry:** What?

**Kramer:** If my umbrella hedge fails in sun, and my hat business fails in rain, I need something that works in both! Something weather-proof!

**Newman:** What's weather-proof?

**Kramer:** *(Eyes widening)* ...Hot dogs! Everyone eats hot dogs! Rain or shine! Hot dogs are the universal constant!

**Jerry:** You're going to buy hot dogs now? Where are you going to put hot dogs? In the hats? Under the umbrellas?

**Kramer:** I'll get a cart! A hot dog cart! I'll park it outside and sell to the hat shoppers and the umbrella shoppers! It's the perfect hedge!

**Newman:** You don't have any money for a hot dog cart. You owe me money.

**Kramer:** I'll use the hats as collateral! Or the umbrellas! Someone will lend against them!

**Jerry:** You're gonna try to get a hot dog cart loan secured by 1980s ladies' hats and promotional plumbing umbrellas. I can't wait to see that loan application.

**Kramer:** *(Already heading for the door, climbing over inventory)* I'm going to the bank! The hot dog hedge is going to save me! This time it's foolproof!

*(Kramer exits. They hear a massive crash as he knocks over a tower of hats and umbrellas in the hall.)*

**Newman:** He's never going to sell a single hot dog.

**Jerry:** Of course not. But he'll be fully hedged against a scenario that doesn't exist. And in Kramer's world, that's called success.

**Newman:** What do I do about my money?

**Jerry:** Congratulations, Newman. You're now an unsecured creditor in the Kramer bankruptcy. Welcome to the club. We meet whenever Kramer bursts through a door.

**Newman:** *(Looking around at the hats and umbrellas)* I'm going to take ten hats today. And three umbrellas. For emotional damages.

**Jerry:** Take the lobster one. It's been watching me for months. I want it gone.

*(Newman starts climbing into the hat mountain as we fade out.)*
