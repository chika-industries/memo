### Scene 3: Goodwill (as an intangible asset)

**Setting:** Kramer's apartment. Newman is there. They are both staring at a small, dusty box on the table.

**Newman:** Are you sure about this, Kramer? It seems... unsavory.

**Kramer:** It's brilliant, Newman! It's all about accounting! When one company buys another, they don't just pay for the buildings and the trucks. They pay for something called "goodwill"!

**Newman:** Goodwill? Like, the feeling?

**Kramer:** No, no. It's an *asset*, Newman! It's the value of the reputation, the customer loyalty, the good name! And I've found a way to cash in on it.

**Newman:** *(Leaning in)* Go on.

**Kramer:** *(Opens the box, revealing a bunch of hand-painted, slightly crooked ceramic frogs)* Behold! "Kramerica's Kreative Keramics"!

**Newman:** They're... hideous.

**Kramer:** They're *assets*! Now, I have no reputation. I have no customer loyalty. My good name is... well, it's Kramer. So my "goodwill" is zero. But! If I can get someone *else* to buy my company...

**Newman:** Who would buy this... company?

**Kramer:** *(Leaning in, conspiratorially)* My across-the-hall neighbor, Jerry Seinfeld. He's a famous comedian! He has tremendous goodwill! If he buys my frog company, even for a dollar, then on his books, he can say he paid $1 for the frogs... and $1,000 for the "goodwill" of Kramerica! It's an asset, Newman! He can write it off! *(There's that phrase again.)*

**Newman:** Wait, wait, wait. He pays you a dollar for the frogs, but claims on his taxes he paid $1,001 total, with $1,000 being for "goodwill"? That seems like he's just... making up a number.

**Kramer:** It's not making it up! It's the value of being associated with me! My ideas! My zest! My hair! That's worth at least a thousand bucks to a guy like Jerry. It's an intangible asset, but it's there! You can feel it!

**Newman:** I feel... confused. And a little bit hungry. But mostly confused.

**Kramer:** It's genius! Jerry gets a tax benefit, I get a thousand bucks, and you, Newman, get... a frog. *(He hands Newman the ugliest frog.)*

**Newman:** *(Looking at the frog)* This feels more like a liability.
