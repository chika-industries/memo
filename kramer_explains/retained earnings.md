
### Scene 15: Retained Earnings

**Setting:** Monk's Diner. Jerry is eating. George enters, looking smug, carrying a small notebook.

**George:** Jerry. I've had an epiphany. A financial revelation. I'm not a failure—I'm a *conservator of capital*.

**Jerry:** You're what now?

**George:** *(Sliding into booth, opening notebook)* Retained earnings. That's the key. It's not about how much you make—it's about how much you keep! Companies do it! They earn profits, then they *retain* them instead of paying them out! That's how you build equity!

**Jerry:** You have retained earnings? From what? Your salary at a job you complain about constantly?

**George:** Exactly! I've been working for years! I've earned money! And what have I done with it? I've retained it! Well, most of it. Some of it I spent. But the principle stands!

**Jerry:** How much have you retained? Total? Lifetime?

**George:** *(Consulting notebook)* According to my calculations... approximately $4,700.

**Jerry:** That's it? That's your lifetime retained earnings? $4,700?

**George:** It's not nothing! It's in a savings account earning 0.2% interest! It's growing! Slowly! Imperceptibly! But it's *retained*, Jerry!

**Jerry:** George, that's not retained earnings. That's just having a small amount of money that you haven't spent yet. Retained earnings are cumulative profits that a business reinvests. You're not a business. You're a man with $4,700 and a lot of opinions.

**George:** I'm a business! My name is a brand! "George Costanza" is an enterprise! And my retained earnings represent years of discipline! Years of saying no to fancy coffee! Years of eating at my parents' house to save money!

**Jerry:** That's not discipline. That's being cheap and mooching off your parents.

*(Kramer bursts in, carrying a briefcase.)*

**Kramer:** Gentlemen! I heard the phrase "retained earnings" from across the diner and I knew I had to join! Because I, too, have been retaining!

**Jerry:** You've been retaining hats. We know.

**Kramer:** No, no! Cash! Actual cash! I've been putting away $5 a week for the last month! That's $20 of retained earnings!

**George:** See? Kramer gets it! We're building equity!

**Jerry:** You're building a pile of $20. That's not equity, that's just... $20.

**Kramer:** But it's *retained* $20! It's the principle! Companies show their strength through retained earnings! It means they're profitable and responsible!

**George:** Exactly! So when my father asks about my finances, I can say, "Frank, my retained earnings stand at $4,700. I'm a sound investment."

**Jerry:** Your father's gonna say, "That's it? After 20 years of working, you've retained $4,700? My barber retains more than that in lint."

**George:** He doesn't understand modern finance! Retained earnings are patient! They wait! They grow!

**Kramer:** Mine are gonna grow too! I'm gonna take my $20 and invest it in something with high returns!

**Jerry:** Please don't say hats.

**Kramer:** I wasn't gonna say hats. I was gonna say... hat accessories!

**Jerry:** That's worse.

---

**Setting:** Jerry's apartment. One month later. George is sitting dejected. Kramer bursts in, furious.

**Kramer:** My retained earnings! Gone! Wiped out! Reduced to nothing!

**Jerry:** What happened? Did hat accessories not pan out?

**Kramer:** I bought a lottery ticket! Mega Millions! $20! I was gonna turn my retained earnings into *massive* earnings! But I didn't win! Not even a free ticket! My retained earnings are now retained losses!

**George:** *(Morosely)* I know how you feel. My father found out about my retained earnings.

**Jerry:** Let me guess. He was unimpressed.

**George:** He laughed, Jerry. He laughed for five minutes. Then he asked if that $4,700 included the money I owe him from 1998. I had forgotten about that money. It's $600. My retained earnings are now $4,100.

**Jerry:** So your father just declared a dividend.

**George:** A hostile dividend! He took it right out of my retained earnings! Said it was "interest on the principle of being a disappointment"!

**Kramer:** The system is rigged, Jerry! We retain earnings, and the world finds ways to take them! It's like the universe has a claim on our equity!

**Jerry:** Or maybe—and hear me out—maybe you shouldn't consider your meager savings as "retained earnings" because that makes it sound like you have a thriving business when really you just have a few dollars and bad ideas.

**George:** So what do I call it? My "sad little pile"?

**Jerry:** That's accurate. "George's Sad Little Pile." It's honest.

**Kramer:** *(Perking up)* Unless... unless we combine our retained earnings! Pool our resources! Form a conglomerate!

**George:** A conglomerate? You and me?

**Kramer:** Costanza-Kramer Enterprises! We'll have combined retained earnings of $4,120! That's real money!

**Jerry:** That's not real money. That's George's money minus what he owes his father, plus your lottery losses. You're starting a business with negative momentum.

**George:** What would we even do?

**Kramer:** *(Pacing)* I've been thinking about this. Hot dogs, remember? Weather-proof business! But with your retained earnings, we could get a cart! A real one! Not just a dream!

**George:** You want me to invest my entire life savings in a hot dog cart with you?

**Kramer:** It's not investing! It's deploying retained earnings! It's what companies do!

**Jerry:** George, do not give Kramer your money. You will never see it again. It will become hats. Or umbrella accessories. Or lottery tickets.

**George:** *(Conflicted)* But... retained earnings are supposed to work for you! They're supposed to generate returns!

**Kramer:** Exactly! Let them work, George! Let them sweat! Let them become hot dogs!

**Jerry:** Your money doesn't sweat. Only you will, when you realize you're now a partner in a hot dog cart with a man who once tried to pay for dinner with a hat.

**George:** *(Long pause)* ...I need to think about this. Retained earnings are precious. They're years of suffering. I can't just... deploy them lightly.

**Kramer:** Think fast, George! The hot dog market waits for no man! While you're thinking, someone else is grilling!

*(Kramer exits. George stares at his notebook.)*

**George:** What do I do, Jerry?

**Jerry:** You retain your earnings. That's what you're good at. You retain them forever, in a sad little account, and you look at the number when you need to feel like you've accomplished something. That's your whole thing.

**George:** *(Nodding slowly)* Yeah. Yeah, you're right. I'll keep retaining. Maybe one day I'll have $5,000. That's the dream.

**Jerry:** A beautiful dream. Truly inspirational.
