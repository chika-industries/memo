### Scene 5: Accounts Receivable vs. Cash

**Setting:** Monk's Diner. Jerry is eating. Kramer rushes in, beaming.

**Kramer:** Jerry! I'm a genius! I'm richer than I thought!

**Jerry:** Did you finally sell that chicken that you've been raising in your apartment?

**Kramer:** No, no. Better. I did a favor for Newman. I walked his dog for a week. He owes me $50.

**Jerry:** So you have $50.

**Kramer:** *(Proudly)* According to my new accounting system... I have $50! I put it right in my ledger under "Accounts Receivable"!

**Jerry: So you **don't** have $50. You have an IOU from Newman. That's not the same thing.**

**Kramer:** Jerry, Jerry, Jerry. You're thinking like an amateur. Accounts Receivable is an asset! It's money that is owed to me! It's as good as cash!

**Jerry:** It is not as good as cash. Cash is in your pocket. Accounts Receivable is in Newman's pocket. There's a big difference. Newman's pocket is a dark, frightening place where promises go to die.

**Kramer:** Details! On my balance sheet, I am $50 richer. I'm thinking of going out to celebrate. Maybe buy that new shirt I saw.

**Jerry:** With what? Newman's promise? Go try to pay for that shirt with "Accounts Receivable." See how that works. "Here's a slip of paper that says my fat, mail-carrier friend might give me money at some undefined point in the future. Do we have a deal?"

**Kramer:** *(Confident)* Businesses do it all the time! It's called credit!

**Jerry:** No, it's called delusion. When you actually get the $50 from Newman, *then* you'll have $50. What you have now is a hope and a prayer and a very smelly dog leash.

*(Just then, Newman walks by the diner window. He sees Kramer, waves cheerfully, and keeps walking. Kramer's face falls slightly.)*

**Kramer:** He's walking pretty fast.

**Jerry:** He's walking away from your "asset." You might want to reclassify that as "Accounts Unreceivable." I think they call that a write-off.
