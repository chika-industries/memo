### Scene 11: LIFO vs FIFO

**Setting:** Kramer's apartment. The fridge is open. Kramer is staring into it intensely, holding a clipboard. The hat situation has been somewhat contained, but now there are groceries everywhere.

**Setting:** Jerry's apartment. Kramer bursts in, clipboard waving.

**Kramer:** Jerry! I've been living a lie! A costly, rotten lie!

**Jerry:** What is it? Did you finally realize that the hats are never going to sell?

**Kramer:** No, no, the hats are fine. It's the *food*, Jerry! The food! I've been doing my inventory all wrong!

**Jerry:** Your inventory? You have a jar of pickles from 2019 and a box of baking soda that you think is seasoning.

**Kramer:** Exactly! And it's because I've been using the wrong accounting method! I need to choose between LIFO and FIFO!

**Jerry:** LIFO and FIFO? Those are inventory costing methods. For businesses. You're not a business. You're a man with a dirty fridge.

**Kramer:** Every man is a business, Jerry! His body is the store! His stomach is the customer! And his refrigerator is the warehouse! Now, listen. FIFO stands for "First In, First Out." That means you sell the oldest inventory first. You use the oldest stuff before it goes bad.

**Jerry:** That... that's actually sensible. That's what normal people do.

**Kramer:** It's *conservative*! It's for accountants! It's safe! It's boring! It's what my mother does!

**Jerry:** So what's LIFO?

**Kramer:** *(Eyes lighting up)* LIFO! "Last In, First Out"! You use the newest stuff first and leave the old stuff in the back! It's aggressive! It's bold! It's *me*, Jerry!

**Jerry:** Why would anyone do that? The old stuff just sits there and rots.

**Kramer:** For the *tax advantages*! In times of rising prices, LIFO reduces your taxable income because you're matching current higher costs against current revenue! It's a wealth-building strategy!

**Jerry:** You're not selling the food. You're eating it. There's no revenue. There's no taxable income. There's just you, eating a sandwich.

**Kramer:** But the *principle*, Jerry! The principle! I've decided to convert my entire personal food inventory to LIFO effective immediately.

**Jerry:** So you're going to eat the fresh stuff first and leave the old stuff to die in the back of your fridge.

**Kramer:** That's right! I'm gonna live in the now! The future can take care of itself!

**Jerry:** The future is gonna open your fridge one day and be murdered by whatever is growing in there.

**Kramer:** *(Ignoring him, consulting his clipboard)* According to my inventory, I have a container of yogurt that's been in there since April. Under FIFO, I'd have to eat that today. But under LIFO, I can ignore it and eat this beautiful, fresh Greek yogurt I bought yesterday!

**Jerry:** That yogurt from April is now a science experiment. It's not yogurt anymore. It's a petri dish with a cult following.

**Kramer:** I'll deal with it later! Right now, I'm gonna go enjoy my fresh yogurt, guilt-free, thanks to LIFO accounting!

*(Kramer exits. Jerry shakes his head.)*

---

**Setting:** Kramer's apartment. One week later. The smell is noticeable even from the hallway.

**Setting:** Jerry's apartment. Kramer bursts in, looking green around the gills, holding his stomach.

**Jerry:** Whoa. You look like death warmed over. And not the good kind, like a celebrity biopic.

**Kramer:** *(Groaning)* LIFO... LIFO is a lie, Jerry.

**Jerry:** What happened? Did the April yogurt finally stage a revolt?

**Kramer:** Worse. I kept using LIFO all week. Fresh milk, fresh eggs, fresh everything. But yesterday, I ran out of fresh bread. I had to reach to the back of the bread box.

**Jerry:** Let me guess. There was bread from the Carter administration back there.

**Kramer:** It was green, Jerry. Green and... fuzzy. Like a Muppet. But I was hungry! So I scraped off the green parts—

**Jerry:** You scraped mold off bread? That's not how mold works! The roots go all the way through!

**Kramer:** I know that NOW! *(Clutches stomach)* I've been... visited. Multiple times. By the ghost of bread past.

**Jerry:** So LIFO didn't work out.

**Kramer:** LIFO is for companies that don't have to eat their inventory! For a human being, FIFO is the only way! You have to rotate your stock! You have to respect the chronology!

**Jerry:** So you've seen the light.

**Kramer:** I've seen the inside of my bathroom more times in 24 hours than I've seen my own face. Yes, I've seen the light.

**Jerry:** Well, welcome to FIFO. It's the boring, sensible choice that keeps you alive.

**Kramer:** *(Weakly)* I'm gonna go home and implement a strict FIFO system. Everything gets dated. Everything gets rotated. I'm gonna be the most organized man in New York.

**Jerry:** And the hats?

**Kramer:** The hats are on LIFO. Nobody wants the old ones anyway. They just sit there. It's perfect.

**Jerry:** Of course. The hats get the bad accounting method. That's only fair.

*(Kramer shuffles out, still holding his stomach.)*

**Jerry:** *(To himself)* FIFO for food, LIFO for hats. Finally, a balanced portfolio.
