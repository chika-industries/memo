### Scene 1: The Parking Ticket

**Setting:** Jerry's apartment. Jerry is looking out the window. Kramer bursts through the door, triumphant.

**Kramer:** It's all taken care of, Jerry! The system worked!

**Jerry:** What system? What are you talking about?

**Kramer:** I got a parking ticket. Fifty bucks! For being twelve minutes late to the meter. A travesty of justice! But I'm not paying it.

**Jerry:** Oh, you're not gonna pay it? And what, you're gonna flee the country? Become a fugitive from the meter maid?

**Kramer:** No, no, no. I'm gonna write it off.

**Jerry:** (Pauses, turns from window) You're going to write off a parking ticket.

**Kramer:** That's right. I'm gonna go down to City Hall, find the right department, and I'm just gonna write it off.

**Jerry:** You think there's a department at City Hall called "Write-Offs"? You just go in and say, "Kramer, 129 West 81st. Got a ticket. I'd like to write it off, please."

**Kramer:** Jerry, the city has a massive budget. Trillions of dollars! My fifty bucks? A rounding error. It's a write-off for them. They won't even miss it. I'm just helping them with their accounting.

**Jerry:** But it's not *for* them! You owe them money! It's not their loss, it's your debt! If you don't pay it, it becomes a loss for *you* when they come and take your knees.

**Kramer:** (Waving him off, heading for the door) Nah, they understand. Big organizations, they think big picture. You gotta think like them, Jerry. Write-offs all the way down.

### Scene 2: The Expired Coupon

**Setting:** Monk's Diner. Jerry and Elaine are at a booth. Kramer walks over, dejected, holding a piece of paper.

**Kramer:** (Slams the paper on the table) It's a travesty!

**Elaine:** What now? Did the market run out of beets again?

**Kramer:** It's a coupon! A coupon for 50% off any large pizza at Mario's Pizzeria. And it expired... yesterday.

**Jerry:** Ah, the old expired coupon tragedy. A real blow to the economy.

**Kramer:** But I already ordered the pizza, Jerry! I used the coupon! I showed it to Mario myself. He said, "Kramer, this expired yesterday." So I paid full price. Eighteen dollars!

**Elaine:** So what's the problem? The coupon expired. You paid. That's how it works.

**Kramer:** No, no, you don't get it. I'm out eighteen dollars that I shouldn't have had to pay. So I'm going back to Mario's to write it off.

**Jerry:** (To Elaine) He's going to write off an expired coupon. (To Kramer) How? How does that work?

**Kramer:** Look, Mario runs a business. He has to account for things. Spoiled cheese, broken ovens, deliveries that go to the wrong address... and expired coupons that people still try to use. It's a write-off for him! A cost of doing business! So he should write it off and give me my eighteen dollars back. It's just good business, Jerry.

**Elaine:** So you want him to give you money for something he doesn't have to give you money for, by claiming it's a business loss that he would then not have to pay taxes on, which still doesn't get you your money back?

**Kramer:** (Stares blankly at Elaine, then at Jerry) See? She gets it.

**Jerry:** No, she just explained why you're wrong.

**Kramer:** (Confident) Details. I'm going to Mario's. (He exits the diner)

**Elaine:** He's going to get beaten up by an Italian pizza chef.

**Jerry:** I'd pay to see that. That's a write-off I could get behind.

### Scene 3: The Broken Gift

**Setting:** Kramer's apartment. Kramer is on the phone. The place is a mess. A beautiful, but clearly broken, ceramic vase is on the table.

**Kramer:** (Into phone) No, no, Newman, it's fine! It's a write-off! ... What do you mean, who's writing it off? The universe, Newman! The universe! ... No, it's a universal principle! Look, I'll call you back.

(He hangs up as Jerry enters, holding a small bag of groceries.)

**Jerry:** What's a universal principle? And why is there a crime scene in your apartment?

**Kramer:** My aunt gave me that vase. A family heirloom. Hand-painted. From the old country.

**Jerry:** It's beautiful. What happened to it?

**Kramer:** I was showing it to Newman. He backed into the table. It's in three pieces. But I figured it out.

**Jerry:** You figured out how to un-break it?

**Kramer:** No! I'm gonna write it off! My aunt, she's old. She's gonna write it off as a loss. "Gave Kramer a vase, he broke it." It's off her books. She can stop worrying about it.

**Jerry:** Your aunt doesn't have books, Kramer! She has a shoe box with receipts from the '70s and a jar of coins. She's not gonna write it off.

**Kramer:** But it clears the slate! The vase is gone. We accept the loss. It's a cosmic write-off. It's about emotional accounting, Jerry. You write it off in your heart. You don't let the past hold you down.

**Jerry:** (Stunned) That... that was actually almost profound.

**Kramer:** (Puffing up with pride) Well, sure.

**Jerry:** For about three seconds. You still have a broken vase and a clumsy friend. You didn't *do* anything.

**Kramer:** I did the most important thing. I made peace with it. Now, I'm gonna go ask my aunt for a different heirloom. A painting, maybe. This one will be on a shelf far away from Newman.

**Jerry:** That's not a write-off! That's just a bold, terrible plan!
