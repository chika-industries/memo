### Scene 12: Leverage

**Setting:** Jerry's apartment. Jerry is at the kitchen counter, making coffee. Kramer bursts in, holding a stack of papers and looking electric with excitement.

**Kramer:** Jerry! I did it! I cracked the code! The rich guys weren't hiding it after all—it was right there in plain sight!

**Jerry:** What code? The secret to not bursting through doors like the Kool-Aid Man?

**Kramer:** No! Leverage! It's the key to everything! You don't need your own money to get rich—you just need other people's money! That's leverage!

**Jerry:** Oh, this sounds dangerous already.

**Kramer:** Listen! Why do the rich get richer? Because they borrow money at 3% and invest it at 10%! They pocket the difference! It's free money, Jerry! Free!

**Jerry:** It's not free. It's borrowed. There's a difference. One involves paying people back.

**Kramer:** Details! I've got it all figured out. I'm gonna use leverage to finally make the hats work!

**Jerry:** The hats? The hats that nobody has bought in six months? The hats that are currently stacked so high in your apartment that you can't even open your fridge all the way?

**Kramer:** Those very hats! See, my problem was I only had $200 of my own money in the hat business. That's equity, Jerry. Small thinking. But if I can borrow $1,000 against the hats, I can buy MORE hats! A bigger inventory! Economies of scale!

**Jerry:** You want to borrow money to buy more of the thing you can't sell.

**Kramer:** Exactly! That's leverage! I'm amplifying my position!

**Jerry:** You're amplifying your problem. You currently have hats you can't sell. If you buy more hats, you'll have MORE hats you can't sell. That's not leverage, that's multiplication of failure.

**Kramer:** No, no, you don't get it. With a bigger inventory, I can attract bigger buyers. Department stores! Costume shops! Theaters doing period pieces about the 1980s! They won't return my calls for 200 hats, but for 2,000 hats? They'll have to take me seriously!

**Jerry:** Who's gonna lend you money? The Hat Fairy?

**Kramer:** *(Pulls out papers)* I've prepared a loan application for the bank! Look! "Kramerica Hat Industries"! Projected revenues: $40,000! Collateral: 2,000 vintage hats! Loan amount requested: $1,000!

**Jerry:** You're gonna walk into a bank, show them a picture of a lobster hat, and ask for a thousand dollars?

**Kramer:** It's not just a picture! I brought a sample! *(Pulls a hideous bright pink hat with netting and plastic fruit from behind his back)*

**Jerry:** Get that thing away from me. It's giving me financial advice I don't want.

**Kramer:** The bank manager is gonna see this and say, "Kramer, this is the future. Here's your money, plus a bonus for your vision."

**Jerry:** The bank manager is gonna say, "Sir, this is a Chase branch. Please leave before I call security."

**Kramer:** You underestimate the power of leverage, Jerry. It's not about what you have—it's about what you can *control*!

---

**Setting:** Monk's Diner. One week later. Jerry and Elaine are at a booth. Kramer slides in, looking shell-shocked and defeated.

**Elaine:** Whoa. What happened to you? You look like you just watched someone eat your last hat.

**Kramer:** *(Hollowly)* The bank said no.

**Jerry:** Shocking. Truly shocking.

**Kramer:** But then... Newman said yes.

**Jerry:** *(Pauses mid-coffee sip)* Newman? Newman lent you money?

**Kramer:** $500. At 15% interest. He called it a "friends and family rate."

**Elaine:** Friends and family? You're not friends. You're co-conspirators in annoyance.

**Kramer:** I thought it was perfect! Leverage! I took Newman's $500, added it to my remaining $100, and bought 3,000 more hats from a guy in New Jersey!

**Jerry:** So you now have... approximately 5,000 hats?

**Kramer:** 5,247. I counted last night. They're everywhere, Jerry. Everywhere. I can't find my toaster. I can't find my bed. I'm sleeping on a pile of hats like some kind of hat dragon.

**Elaine:** A hat dragon that owes Newman $500 at 15%.

**Kramer:** It gets worse. The guy in New Jersey? His hats are... different.

**Jerry:** Different how?

**Kramer:** *(Pulls out a hat from under the table—it's a cheap-looking baseball cap with a terrible logo)* They're not fancy ladies' hats. They're promotional hats from a failed plumbing supply company in the 1990s. "Pete's Plumbing - We Fix Leaks!" with a picture of a cartoon plunger.

**Jerry:** So you now have 5,000 fancy ladies' hats that nobody wants, plus 3,000 plumbing promotional hats that nobody has ever wanted.

**Kramer:** My leverage backfired, Jerry. I amplified my losses. Newman wants his money by Friday or he says he's gonna start taking hats. One hat per day until the debt is paid.

**Elaine:** That's... actually kind of fair. He's gonna collect his collateral.

**Kramer:** At one hat per day, it'll take him 14 years! He's gonna be in my apartment every morning, picking out a hat! My life is gonna be a nightmare of Newman hat selections!

**Jerry:** You know what they call leverage when it goes wrong?

**Kramer:** What?

**Jerry:** Debt. Just regular old debt. You've discovered the downside of other people's money—you have to give it back.

**Kramer:** *(Head in hands)* I'm leveraged to Newman. The most dangerous position of all.

**Elaine:** Can you sell any of the plumbing hats?

**Kramer:** I tried! I wore one on the street and a guy came up and asked if I could fix his toilet. When I said no, he got angry. Said I was "false advertising." I'm lucky to be alive.

**Jerry:** So you're leveraged, you're losing money on every hat, and your lender is Newman, who sleeps 20 feet from your door.

**Kramer:** *(Standing up, a flicker of his old energy)* Unless... what if I get MORE leverage? What if I borrow from someone else to pay Newman, then use the extra to buy even MORE hats, and then the sheer volume of hats will create a hat singularity that collapses the market and I emerge as the sole hat king?!

**Jerry:** That's not leverage. That's a pyramid scheme. With hats.

**Elaine:** It's a hat pyramid. A hatramid.

**Kramer:** *(Grabbing his plumbing hat)* I'm gonna go see a guy about a loan. A tough guy. A guy who understands the hat business.

**Jerry:** You're gonna borrow from a mobster now?

**Kramer:** It's not a mobster! It's a private equity enthusiast! He just happens to have very strong opinions about repayment!

*(Kramer exits.)*

**Elaine:** How long until he's sleeping in a hat box in the sewer?

**Jerry:** I give it a week. Maybe two if the hats keep him warm.

**Elaine:** You know what his problem is?

**Jerry:** What?

**Elaine:** He used leverage without any earnings. It's like buying a mansion with no income. Eventually, the bank comes for the mansion.

**Jerry:** In his case, the bank is Newman, and the mansion is made of hats. This is going to end beautifully.
